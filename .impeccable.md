# Design Context

## Target Audience
- Korean-speaking Christians (church members, pastors, worship leaders)
- Used primarily during church services, Bible study groups, and personal devotion
- All ages, but must be readable by older adults (larger fonts)

## Use Cases
1. **Bible Reading**: Read Korean (개역개정) and English (NKJV) Bible side by side
2. **Presentation Mode**: Display Bible verses on a projector/large screen during worship services - ONE verse at a time, very large text
3. **Verse Search**: Quickly find specific passages (e.g., "민수기 3장 4-10절", "요 3:16")
4. **Navigation**: Browse through books, chapters, and verses

## Brand Personality / Tone
- **Reverent & Sacred**: This is a Bible app - it should feel dignified and respectful
- **Warm & Inviting**: Not cold or sterile. Use warm tones (gold, cream, warm browns)
- **Clean & Readable**: Readability is the #1 priority. Large, clear typography
- **Timeless**: Avoid trendy UI patterns. Classic, enduring design
- **Dark theme preferred**: Easier on eyes during worship services in dimly lit sanctuaries

## Key Design Requirements
- **Font Size Priority**: Text must be LARGE. Default reading font 20px+, presentation mode should fill the screen
- **Presentation Mode**: Full-screen, dark background, centered verse text that auto-scales to fill screen without clipping
- **Bilingual Display**: Korean text prominent, English (NKJV) as secondary/italic
- **High Contrast**: Must be readable on projectors and large screens
- **Mobile Responsive**: Works on phones, tablets, and desktop/projector

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Refined & Editorial**: Like a beautifully typeset Bible, not a tech app
- **Warm Dark Theme**: Deep charcoal/near-black with warm gold accents
- **Serif for Scripture**: Use a quality serif font for Bible text to evoke traditional print Bibles
- **Generous Whitespace**: Let the text breathe
